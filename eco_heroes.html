Eco Heroes

Leo Sordello-Savale

Age 8
London, United Kingdom
Leo’s Animal Planet

Eight-year-old Leo, creator of "Leo’s Animal Planet" on YouTube, boasts 1,100 subscribers and 100,000 views. Leo's videos, ranging from sharks to bees, entertain and educate on animal conservation. Driven by lockdown boredom, Leo and his mother produce engaging content to raise awareness about endangered species and climate change. His storytelling, facts, and interviews with experts make his videos globally popular and educational tools in schools. Leo's mission is to inspire people to protect nature. His work garnered praise from David Attenborough, UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson, and awards, including the Young Animal Hero Award in 2022 and the CREST Young Stem Award in 2023.

Eiha Dixit

Age 9
Meerut, Uttar Pradesh, India
Green Eiha Smile Foundation

Eiha, starting at age four, leads the Green Eiha Smile Foundation, transforming her home into an environmental hub. Her group has planted 20,000+ saplings using water-efficient methods, creating green spaces in Indian cities. Beyond planting, they make seed balls from fruit seeds and repurpose donated plants into a plant bank, offering free resources. Eiha recycles single-use plastic bottles into plant holders. Recognized globally, Eiha spoke at the UN's World Peace Summit 2022, receiving accolades like the Prime Minister National Child Award and Water Hero Award. As a brand ambassador, she advocates for Mission100CroreTree and Clean India, Green India. Eiha's mission: "Beautify the earth with greenery while we're alive," committed to planting trees until her last breath.

Manya Harsha

Age 12
Bangalore, India
Youth for Climate Action

At 13, Manya is a passionate environmentalist, poet, and author. Inspired by nature and driven by climate concerns, she has written seven environmental books and runs The Little Environmentalist YouTube channel. Since 2020, she publishes SUNSHINE, a monthly youth magazine promoting climate action. Manya actively engages in environmental activities like planting saplings, distributing sustainable bags, and organizing clean-up drives. Despite her accomplishments, she remains humble, emphasizing the importance of small actions. Her focus is on creating a better future for the next generation.

Karnav Rastogi

Age 13
Andheri West, Mumbai, India
Each One, Read One to Plant One

Inspired by nature, author and poet Karnav Rastogi joined the Tide Turner Plastic Challenge, aiming to reduce plastic waste. He created two e-books on climate change, positively impacting students and adults. Realizing some lacked internet access, he distributed 5,000 hard copies of his latest book, “Kartik, Mixie & Climate Change,” initiating “Each One, Read One to Plant One.” The book includes a green pledge and a neem seed packet, promoting tree planting. Karnav aims to distribute 10,000 copies in multiple languages, emphasizing the role of students as champions of change to raise climate awareness.

Nirvaan Somany

Age 16
New Delhi, India
Project Jeans – Blue to Green

Shocked by the environmental impact of denim production, Nirvaan decided to address the issue. Learning that used jeans contribute to landfill waste, he repurposed them into washable and insulating sleeping bags for the homeless. With 6,000 collected pairs, he educated through social media, organized drop-offs, and distributed bags through NGOs. Nirvaan's initiative, born out of concern for the planet and the underprivileged, has provided 800 individuals with practical and sustainable sleeping solutions.

Mannat Kaur

Age 15
New Delhi, India
Indigenous Greywater Home Treatment Plan

Mannat, a young scientist, addresses water scarcity and carbon emissions by designing a system to collect and filter greywater for reuse in households. Her invention, consisting of Bilge Vessel (BV) and Scupper Valve (SV), is a compact, smart, and low-cost solution that fits seamlessly into existing plumbing. If adopted by 20% of homes in her city, it could save thousands of liters of fresh water daily and reduce operational costs. Mannat has presented her innovation at global forums, emphasizing the decentralization of wastewater treatment and showcasing her commitment to making a positive impact on the world.

Satvika Iyer

Age 16
Milpitas,California,USA
Plant Nutrition & Waste Management of School Food Systems

During the COVID-19 pandemic, 8th-grader Satvika Iyer noticed issues in school lunches. In high school, she advocated for plant-based nutrition and waste management, successfully implementing a plant-based menu in her school district. She influenced the Healthy Meals, Healthy Kids Act, and shared her vision at the White House. Satvika now aims to assist other districts in adopting sustainable food systems and advocating for legislation to reduce food waste.

Rahul Vijayan

Age 16
Pearland, Texas, USA
Farm to Tray

Rahul noticed a nutrition problem in public schools, where unhealthy frozen meals were contributing to childhood obesity. He founded the non-profit Farm to Tray, introducing hydroponic farming to provide fresh organic produce. Starting at Shadowcreek High School, his 4x6-foot hydroponic system became a model for 18 Houston schools. Supported by grants, he expanded to California and New York. Rahul hosted a successful hydroponic symposium, leading to a collaboration with Houston Methodist Hospital to use hydroponic kits as portable healing gardens for oncology patients. His initiative has earned praise for reimagining childhood nutrition in public schools.
